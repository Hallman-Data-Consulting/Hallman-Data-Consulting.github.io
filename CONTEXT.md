# Hallman Data Consulting — Company Website

## Project Purpose
This is the official company website for **Hallman Data Consulting**, a freelance data analytics consultancy. The site serves as a marketing and credibility tool for prospective small business clients — not a personal resume or developer portfolio.

---

## Business Overview
- **Company:** Hallman Data Consulting
- **Owner:** Levi Hallman, Indianapolis IN (data science masters student)
- **Service Model:** Build custom dashboards, models, and data pipelines for small businesses. Charge a monthly retainer for ongoing upkeep and updates.
- **Stage:** Portfolio/demo phase, actively working toward first paying client
- **GitHub Org:** https://github.com/Hallman-Data-Consulting

---

## Target Audience
Small business owners who have operational data (sales, customers, bookings, payments) but have never done anything with it. They are **not** technical. Copy and messaging should focus on business outcomes, not technology.

**Speak to problems like:**
- "I don't know which customers are about to leave"
- "I can't tell which products or services are actually making me money"
- "I have data in spreadsheets but no idea what to do with it"

**Avoid jargon like:** RFM analysis, K-Means clustering, ETL pipelines, Pandas, etc.

---

## Site Goals
1. Explain what we do in plain language
2. Show a concrete example of what a deliverable looks like (Ironforge Fitness demo)
3. Communicate the retainer model so prospects understand what they're buying
4. Provide one clear call to action (book a call or send an email)
5. Establish trust and legitimacy

---

## Demo Project — Ironforge Fitness
A synthetic gym client built as a portfolio demo. Shows the full end-to-end product a client would receive.

- **Live dashboard:** http://18.214.89.223:8501
- **GitHub repo:** https://github.com/Hallman-Data-Consulting/ironforge-fitness-example
- **What it shows:** Member segmentation, churn risk identification, revenue trends, class analytics, and individual member lookup
- **Framing for the site:** "Here's what your deliverable could look like" — position it as a real example, not a toy

---

## Site Structure (Planned)
1. **Hero** — Who we are and what problem we solve, single CTA
2. **What We Do** — Plain-language explanation of the service and retainer model
3. **How It Works** — Simple step-by-step (we connect to your data → we build your dashboard → we maintain it monthly)
4. **Demo / Work** — Ironforge Fitness example with live link and short description
5. **About** — Brief bio on Levi, credentials without being resume-like
6. **Contact** — Email and/or contact form

## Tone & Style Guidelines
- **Tone:** Professional but approachable. Confident, not salesy.
- **Audience reading level:** Non-technical business owner
- **Length:** Keep copy tight. No walls of text.
- **Avoid:** Tech jargon, buzzwords, anything that sounds like a developer's resume
